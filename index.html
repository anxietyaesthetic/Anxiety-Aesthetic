♥ About this Page ♥

This page is for all those with any anxiety diagnosis and their severity. It aims to help people understand anxiety better, learn coping methods, and find treatment resources. This site encourages sharing experiences and tips. Remember, conquering anxiety takes time — but you can do it.

♥ When Was Anxiety Discovered? ♥

Historically, anxiety has not always been recognized as a mental disorder. Anxiety is part of our evolution—our fight-or-flight system. In 1980, the American Psychiatric Association officially recognized anxiety disorders. Before that, it was often misdiagnosed as “nerves” or “stress,” with little understanding or effective treatment.

♥ What is Anxiety? ♥

Anxiety is a feeling of fear, dread, and uneasiness about future events. It can include tension, worried thoughts, and physical symptoms. While normal in stressful situations, chronic anxiety can interfere with daily life. Fortunately, it’s treatable with effective therapies and medications.

♥ Symptoms ♥

Symptoms vary, but common signs include:
- Increased heart rate
- Heavy breathing
- Muscle tension
- Fatigue
- Irritability

♥ Types of Anxiety Disorders ♥

Anxiety becomes a disorder when it persists and interferes with life. Common types include:
- Generalized Anxiety Disorder
- Panic Disorder
- Social Anxiety Disorder
- Specific Phobia Disorders

♥ Generalized Anxiety Disorder ♥

GAD involves persistent and excessive worry about many topics. It affects 6.8 million adults in the U.S. each year. Causes may include biological, familial, or experiential factors. Common symptoms: restlessness, fatigue, trouble concentrating, and sleep problems.

♥ Panic Disorder ♥

Panic disorder causes sudden, intense fear attacks with symptoms such as sweating, chest pain, nausea, trembling, or dizziness. Treatment often involves Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), medication, or both.

♥ Social Anxiety Disorder ♥

People with social anxiety disorder experience intense fear of being judged or embarrassed in social situations. It often begins in childhood and may last for years if untreated. Treatment includes therapy and medication.

♥ Specific Phobia Disorders ♥

Specific phobias cause intense fear of certain situations or objects. They can lead to avoidance behavior and sometimes panic attacks. Common examples include acrophobia (heights) and zoophobia (animals).
